About Quinn Evans
Quinn Evans is a nationally recognized award-winning architecture and design firm informed by the past to design our shared future. We are technical experts who act as caretakers, conveners, and listeners able to identify the solutions that will best serve the community and long-term life of a place.  Addressing the complex challenges of a variety of project types  from public sites, museums and visitor centers, civic, performing arts, educational, and hospitality, to multi-family housing - we bring expertise in cultural and sustainable stewardship to every decision in our projects and our practice.
Team building is critical to the Quinn Evans practice. We create better work and more fulfilling professional lives together through a culture that fosters constructive engagement with a diversity of staff, practice, client communities, and points of view.
Be a part of a collaborative and diverse team designing ways for places and people to thrive today and tomorrow. As one of the largest women-owned design practices in the country, Quinn Evans takes pride in our diverse team and workplace culture that emphasizes leadership, innovation, teamwork, and professional development. We offer competitive compensation, a supportive hybrid work environment, and opportunity to make a difference through work on unique and impactful projects.
Our commitment to contributing to and advancing our profession is reflected in sponsorship and team member leadership positions in the Association for Preservation Technology, membership in the Presidents Circle of the National Organization for Minority Architects (NOMA) and as a signatory of the 1.5C COP26 Communiqu.
Job Description
Quinn Evans has an immediate opening for a Full-Time Administrative Assistant in our Baltimore office. This is an exciting opportunity for an independent, ambitious person looking to be part of the success and growth of our firm. The ideal candidate is a team player who is highly organized, detail-oriented with good communication skills. The position requires initiative, flexibility, and a love of multitasking.
Responsibilities


Administrative Support: Provide general administrative support to the team, including scheduling, handling phone calls, emails, correspondence, and greeting visitors.


Office Management: Oversee office organization and tidiness, including maintaining office equipment, office supplies, handling incoming and outgoing mail.


Communication: Act as a liaison between team members, clients, and external partners, ensuring effective communication and providing timely responses to inquiries.


Problem Solving: Handle routine issues independently and escalate more complex problems to the appropriate parties as needed.


Data Entry: Accurately input and maintain data into various systems or databases. This may include client information, financial records, or any other relevant data.


Meeting Support: Assist in the preparation and organization of meetings, including scheduling, sending out invitations, arranging meeting rooms, and preparing necessary materials.


Event Coordination: Help with the planning and execution of company events or conferences, including venue booking, logistics, and coordination.


Calendar Management: Schedule appointments, meetings, and conference calls, and handle any necessary changes or adjustments.


Travel Arrangements: Coordinate travel arrangements, including booking flights, accommodations, and transportation for employees or visitors as required.


Qualifications


Significant proficiencyknowledge of Microsoft Office


Strong organizational, oral, and written communication skills and the ability to be flexible.


Self-starter and team-oriented


Attention to detail and accuracy in work, professional and friendly demeanor, with excellent interpersonal skills.


Flexibility and adaptability to changing priorities and requirements


Highly organized and professional with the ability to prioritize and manage multiple tasks simultaneously
