Robinhoods legal team is looking for an Assistant General Counsel to join our growing Privacy and Data Protection legal team. This role reports directly to our Vice President and Chief Privacy Officer. We are looking for a highly adaptable and collaborative person who can handle ambiguity and rapidly shifting priorities with flexibility and patience. You will help lead our efforts to embed privacy principles in our strategic and tactical processes.This is an exciting opportunity to join a growing company and contribute to our mission to democratize finance for all.
What youll do

Maintain subject matter expertise in areas of privacy and data protection laws (including state and federal consumer protection and privacy laws, GDPR and other global privacy and data protection laws) and advise on their impact on Robinhoods businesses
Provide advice regarding Robinhoods systems and how best to make sure that the enterprise is in compliance
Assist product lawyers in evaluating the impact of privacy and data protection laws, regulatory guidance and enforcement actions, and other related guidelines on product features and support operations
Focus on marketing and adtech, which would include having a developed understanding of mobile apps, interest-based advertising and relevant privacy laws
Maintain expertise in Artificial Intelligence and machine learning in the privacy and fintech context
Maintain expertise in peer-to-peer payment flows and payment ecosystem technologies and their privacy and data security implications
Provide advice and be able to develop, implement and maintain data protection policies and procedures enterprise-wide
Build systems and processes to support scalable privacy program
Draft and oversee the development and management of Robinhoods data policies and practices standards
Develop and conduct general and role-specific privacy trainings
Collaborate closely with the Security and Engineering teams and create effective, cross functional relationships with multiple stakeholders across the enterprise

What you bring 

J.D. degree (or foreign equivalent) and membership in at least 1 U.S. state bar
5 years of legal practice experience specializing in privacy law
Experience with GDPR, CCPA and other privacy laws and regulations
Experience with consumer-facing technology andor financial services companies
Track record of developing practical solutions to novel problems
In-house legal experience
Exceptional project management skills
