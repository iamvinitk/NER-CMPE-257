About the role
The Category Manager is responsible for the design and execution of the category strategy. You are the leader of a business within Collectables, and responsible for its growth and profitability - leveraging your team of experts, departments around you (including marketing and customer success), and commercial strategies to achieve success. 
The goal of the Category manager is to increase the sales and profitability of the category by driving sustainable growth that fits within the Catawikis value proposition, while having PL responsibility. You will need to bring your commercial acumen, and ability to work together with others towards success.
You ensure a constant flow of quality supply through the management of high potential leads and sellers. You work closely with the sales team and your experts to bring in leads, convert them into new high potential sellers, onboard them, and ensure their continued success as customers.
The Category Manager manages an international team of Experts and designs budget and headcount decisions for the category. They facilitate and ensure close collaboration between the category and supporting teams.
What youll do

Overseeing a significant category (based on size, complexity, growth opportunity and or team set up) through a high level of senior leadership
Creating and developing a strategic long term plan for the development of the category in accordance with the Supercluster strategy as set by the Vice President
Analysing data and insights related to demand, supply, quality, productivity and customer experience, in order to improve sustainable growth
Setting the quarterly auction planning by proactively managing supply  demand cycles
Accountability for the category PL
Quality management and curation consistency and efficiency amongst the Experts base
Securing the development of an international team of Experts
Ensuring the category proposition to its buyers and sellers is in line with Catawikis overall value proposition
Ensuring customer centricity within the Customer Journey
Developing strong working relationships with key sellers and buyers
Aligning the strategy across departments and enhancing collaboration amongst the different teams
Analysing and benchmarking key competitors, having an understanding of the drivers and trends in the market

What youll bring to the role

Minimum 5-10 years experience leading a category, setting up the strategy and being able to translate it into actionable KPIs. 
Experience with Supply  Demand planning cycles
Commercial procedures and negotiation strategies aimed at optimising inventories and maximising the category growth
Managing a large team of people, ensuring smooth running of weekly operations - managing teams remotely is a must have
Working in a target-driven environment, motivating teams while ensuring quality standards are met at all times
Collaborative approach with other teams and excellent communication and presentation skills
