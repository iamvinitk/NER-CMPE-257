Supernal is at the forefront of creating emerging mobility solutions that will foster the development of human-centered cities. We are designing a completely new electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft tailored to the mobility needs of future cities. This allows passengers a seamless intermodal journey that safely transports them to their final destination. We fuse research in autonomy, robotics, aviation and services to define a new category of mobility for the worlds communities. We believe in creative thinking and collaboration to help build a better mobility experience for everyone, improving peoples ability to move  whether for work or play.
What we do: 
The Sr. Electric Motor Engineer is responsible for hardware development for the electric motors. This position will work closely with Battery Research  Technology, Vehicle Management Systems, Manufacturing, Quality, and Test  Evaluation teams.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Lead design and development of the electromagnetic components of the electric motor. This includes, but is not limited to, defining motor topology, control interfaces, and circuitry
Assess development risks and coordinate mitigation strategies
Coordinate all electric motor technology maturation activities in order to achieve aircraft certification and entry into service
Lead analysis efforts to include: motor performance, thermal, electromagnetic and mechanical stress, and dynamicsvibrations
Derive system level requirements and specifications based on vehicle Key Performance Parameter (KPPs) and requirements
Define and mature mechanical, electrical, and communication and collaborate with aircraft systems that include airframe, wiring, transmissions, and vehicle management systems
Collaborate with supplierspartners on technical development topics to ensure program achievements are achieved
Work with flight test engineers to define instrumentation requirements and interfaces
Collaborate with motor control team and system architecture and safety team to develop motor control validation and verification plans
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned

What you can contribute:

Bachelors degree in Electrical, Mechanical, Aerospace, or related engineering
A minimum of five (5) years of industry experience in electric motor machine development (an equivalent combination of education and experience may be considered)
Demonstrated ability to effectively manage schedule, budget, suppliers, and product development efforts
Knowledgeable in electromagnetic topologies
Excellent verbal and written communication skills
Proactive delivery of communication and follow up
Excellent organizational skills and attention to detail
Must have the ability to independently prioritize and accomplish work within time constraints
Self-motivated and forward-thinking personality
Proficiency with Simulink or other multi-physics simulation tools
Advanced knowledge of power electronics products for EV powertrain: ACDC converters, battery chargers, inverters, EMI filters, high voltage power distribution and others
Advanced understanding of EMC (electromagnetic compatibility) standards
Advanced understanding of electrical engineering protocols and standards
Advanced knowledge of DVPR and DFMEA

You may also be able to contribute:

Experience with full life-cycle of electric motor development, qualification, production and product support preferred
Experience with motor control hardware andor software development preferred
Experience with 500-1200 VDC systems is preferred
Experience working in aviation industry is preferred
Experience working with regulatory agencies is preferred
