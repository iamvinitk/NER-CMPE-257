Supernal is at the forefront of creating emerging mobility solutions that will foster the development of human-centered cities. We are designing a completely new electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft tailored to the mobility needs of future cities. This allows passengers a seamless intermodal journey that safely transports them to their final destination. We fuse research in autonomy, robotics, aviation and services to define a new category of mobility for the worlds communities. We believe in creative thinking and collaboration to help build a better mobility experience for everyone, improving peoples ability to move  whether for work or play.
What we do: 
The Model Based Software Engineer works with other members of the team to develop all modular application software expected to run on Supernals aircraft management computer, including logic, displays, guidance, navigation, and controls. This position helps develop our test infrastructure, including simulations, and participates in hardware integration. The Model Based Software Engineer will collaborate functionally with colleagues from other teams to support the overall success of the organization.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Participate in the first software build flying our prototype vehicle
Take ownership of one or more avionics applications
For each avionics application, perform the following:

Design the application to comply with requirements and development standards
Write and trace modelscode to avionics systems requirements
Collaborate with hardware and integration groups
Write application simulators to be used during desktop development and in the Integration Lab
Perform requirements-based testing on the desktop and in the Integration Lab
Work with simulated, laboratory, and field test data to debug applications
Create test and simulation tooling to accelerate avionics applications models development


Assist in building a lab for testing and integration of the avionics applications and systems
Additional duties as assigned
