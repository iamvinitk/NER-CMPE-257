What we do: 
The Sr. Powertrain Safety Engineer is responsible for ensuring functional safety requirements from aerospace standardsguidelines and vehicle system teams are well defined and well-integrated with our Powertrain team.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Lead assessment of functional safety issues in the current design
Develop andor lead the reviews of Powertrain functional safety related items during FHA, FMEA, FTA, etc. processes with both suppliers and internal teams
Collaborate with the system safety team to define and improve functional safety concepts
Develop cascading functional safety concepts from system level to develop technical safety requirements for Powertrain systems
Work closely with test and validation teams to develop and improve functional safety related test requirements and methodologies
Contribute to functional safety architecture development for Powertrain system
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned

What you can contribute:

Bachelors degree in Mechanical, Aerospace, or Electrical engineering
A minimum of eight (8) years of related experience in the development, test, and qualification of Powertrain-related systems; at least 3 years experience in development of functional safety artifacts (an equivalent combination of education and experience may be considered for this role)
Professional knowledge of internal combustion, hybrid electric, andor all-electric powerplants (engine, fuel, oil, hydraulics, generator, FADEC, electric motor, motor control unit, power distribution and control, lithium-based energy storage systems)
Experience with functional safety analysis tools such as Windchill FTA, Rhapsody, etc
Experience with requirement management tools such as DOORS, JAMA, etc
Experience with modeling, simulation and validation using MatlabSimulink
Experience developing functional test plans and coordination with test team for test plan execution, data acquisition, data processing, and reporting
Experience with aviation standards such as RTCA DO-178C, DO-254A, and DO-160; and SAE ARP4761 and ARP4754
Familiarity with FAA part 272933
Excellent verbal and written communication skills
Proactive delivery of communication and follow up
Excellent organizational skills and attention to detail
Must have the ability to independently prioritize and accomplish work within time constraints
Advanced knowledge of DVPR and DFMEA

You may also be able to contribute:

Knowledge of rotor and drive systems is a plus
Experience with regulatory agencies is preferred
Experience working in aviationaerospace is preferred
Experience with 500-1200 VDC systems is preferred
Experience with Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) methods and tools is preferred
