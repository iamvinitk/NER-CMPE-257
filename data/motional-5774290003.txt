 
 This is an onsite position located in our Las Vegas Office 
Mission Summary
Motional is looking for a Vehicle Integration Engineer to play a key role in integration testing on our prototype vehicles. From Sensors to Compute, you will be working within a cross functional team on a wide breadth of next generation technology. As we expand our global footprint, we are looking for exceptional engineers to help us set the foundation and direction for the future of autonomous driving.
Are you interested in working with next-generation technology on a team that shares the same passion?
The Motional Las Vegas office is located less than 10 miles from the Las Vegas strip at 730 E Pilot Road, the Las Vegas office is home to one of the largest fleets of self-driving vehicles. The citys grid system of roads and being one of Americas smart cities allows for extensive research and development testing.
What youll be doing:

Repair, maintain, calibrate, test, and validate electrical and mechanical systems
Collaborate with Electrical, Mechanical, and Systems Engineers to develop technical solutions related to the operation and performance of autonomous vehicle systems
Bring-up autonomous vehicle system and verify its functionality and performance to make it suitable for on-road operations
Triage, troubleshoot, and repair failures of autonomous vehicle hardware
Track the status of retrofits and upgrades to vehicles
Perform sensor calibrations, software upgrades, firmware updates, hardware changes
Inspect and maintain equipment using debugging tools such as multimeters, oscilloscopes, and PC-based diagnostic software
Certifying autonomous vehicle readiness for daily RD usage
Perform root-cause analysis with issues presented to the team
Author, update and maintain documentation related to system configuration and current config sets.

What were looking for:

Bachelors degree in Electrical, Computer, Mechatronics Engineering or related field
Able to diagnose and troubleshoot automotive andor robotic systems
Must have experience working in a Linux terminal troubleshooting environment
Capable of working independently to quickly overcome challenges without supervision
Skilled in isolating, documenting, and tracking issues systematically
Ability to troubleshoot computers, software and network infrastructure
Proficient in communicating with other technicians, software engineers, and executives
Proficient utilizing communication and standard office software suites
Proficiency with serialdifferential communication protocols

Bonus points (not required):

Working knowledge of Atlassian feature set including JIRA, Confluence, BitBucket, and Jenkins
Working knowledge of programming languages (JAVA, C, etc)
Proficiency in working with Docker, Python, Wireshark
Knowledge of modern automotive diagnostic procedures and systems
Experienced with computer system architecture, lidars, radars, cameras, CAN bus, and data networks
Proficiency or exposure to Vector CAN tool (CANalyzer, CANape, CANoe etc.)
