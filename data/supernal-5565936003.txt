What we do: 
Our Airframe Structural Analysis  Test team is responsible for performing analysis of airframe structures and works closely with Structural Design, Loads, PayloadsInteriors, Material  Process, Manufacturing Technology, System and Test  Evaluation groups. We are looking for an experienced Stress Analysis Engineer to help build our structural analysis capabilities from the ground up. Our ideal candidate has impressive experience and expertise in structural analysis of airframe primary structures. This position will report to the Airframe Structural Analysis  Test Manager and provide technical solutions for the Airframe Structural Analysis team.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Perform stress analysis of aerospace primary and secondary structures made of composite and metallic materials for experimental and type certificated vehicles
Apply free body diagrams, hand calculations and numerical methods for estimating and developing loads
Develop and apply analysis methods for static strength, stability, durability, and damage tolerance
Support development of new manufacturing technologies, engineering tools, and aircraft structural designs
Work closely with design and test engineers to determine test requirements and support vehicle and sub-scale tests
Coordinate and communicate regularly with team members across Supernal to provide and receive updates on project progress, quality metrics, project summaries, and other related documents
Work with minimal supervision to come up with conceptual and detailed analysis based on the requirements
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned

What you can contribute:

Bachelor or masters degree in Mechanical, Aerospace, Aeronautical, Civil, or equivalent fields
A minimum of three (3) years of experience working with fixed- or rotary-wing aircraft structure strength, stability, fatigue analysis andor finite element analysis (an equivalent combination of education and experience may be considered)
Experience with composites structures
Experience with analytical tools such as HyperX, Nastran, Abaqus, LS-DYNA, Patran, FEMAP, Hyperworks, 3DX, OptiStruct, NASGRO, SlimVision or similar
Excellent verbal and written communication skills
Excellent organizational skills and attention to detail
Must have the ability to independently prioritize and accomplish work within time constraints

You may also be able to contribute:

Familiarity with industry level composite activities (e.g. NCAMP, CMH17 or P17) is desired
Familiarity with durability and damage tolerance analysis techniques for metallic andor non-metallic structure is desired
Familiarity with MRB, structural testing, flight test, or analysis automation (e.g. Python programming or similar) is desired
