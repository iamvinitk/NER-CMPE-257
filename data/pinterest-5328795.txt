Within the Ads Quality team, we try to connect the dots between the aspirations of pinners and the products offered by our partners. The ideal candidate is a strong Economist that understands market design concepts and has the engineering skills to bring them to market. We are looking for an economist who can work side by side with other engineers, to advance the efficiency of the Pinterest Marketplace.
In this role, you will be responsible for developing and executing a vision for the evolution of the Pinterest marketplace. You will design and implement systems that improve the ads delivery funnel, experiments that shape the utility function, auction mechanism design, ad allocation and for deriving new insights through analysis of the marketplace dynamics. In short, this is a unique position, where youll get the freedom to work to bring together pinners and partners in this unique marketplace.
What youll do:

Develop statistical models and systems aimed at improving the marketplace design and operations to maximize value for the Pinners, Partners and Pinterest. This includes engineering solutions for tuning marketplace parameters in the utility function, optimizing ad diversity and ad load across the Pinterest product, auction implementation as well as modeling long-term marketplace effects to minimize ad blindness and maximize advertiser satisfaction.
Build and improve backend systems and statistical models that underlay the marketplace to maximize value for Pinners, Partners and Pinterest.
Define and implement experiments to understand long term Marketplace effects.
Develop strategies to balance long and short term business objectives.
Drive multi-functional collaboration with peers and partners across the company to improve knowledge of marketplace design and operations.

What were looking for:

MS or PhD degree in Computer Science, Statistics or related field.
8 years of industry experience and 2 years of experience leading projectsteams.
Strong software engineering and mathematical skills with knowledge of statistical methods.
Hands-on experience with large-scale online e-commerce systems is a plus.
Background in computational advertising is preferred, but not required.
