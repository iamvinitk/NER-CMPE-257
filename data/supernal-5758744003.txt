Supernal is at the forefront of creating emerging mobility solutions that will foster the development of human-centered cities. We are designing a completely new electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft tailored to the mobility needs of future cities. This allows passengers a seamless intermodal journey that safely transports them to their final destination. We fuse research in autonomy, robotics, aviation and services to define a new category of mobility for the worlds communities. We believe in creative thinking and collaboration to help build a better mobility experience for everyone, improving peoples ability to move  whether for work or play.
What we do: 
Supernal is seeking a Sr. Systems Integration Engineer, Aircraft Components for the overall assembly and integration product team. This role reports directly to the Aircraft Systems Integration Manager and will ensure accurate collaboration across all engineering functional groups. This will involve developing standardized processes and procedures while working closely with the design teams throughout the design phases. The Sr. Systems Integration Engineer, Aircraft Components will also assist in trade studies during the conceptual and preliminary design phases to meet the programs performance, weight, cost, and schedule requirements. Additionally, they will ensure that all aircraft subsystem requirements are met and efficiently packaged within the airframe structure. The Sr. Systems Integration Engineer, Aircraft Components will also be key in building prototype and flight test aircraft and transitioning to production flight vehicles.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Collaborate with various engineering subject areas to design high-reliability, safety-critical systems for an electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft
Zonal management of the digital mock-up for a large section of the aircraft
Control space allocation for all subsystems
Manage routing envelopes for all subsystems
Ensure all subsystems are accessible and maintainable
Manage interface requirements models (IRMs) for their zone
Manage the top aircraft assembly part number and BOM structure
Hold regular design fly-through meetings to resolve disconnects and improve design through CDR
Manage master geometry models for the respective zone
Ensure all system separation requirements are upheld
Collaborate with manufacturing engineering to ensure that the aircraft zones are designed effectively for the manufacturing process
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned
