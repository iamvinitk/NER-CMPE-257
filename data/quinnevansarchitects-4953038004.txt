About Quinn Evans
Quinn Evans is a nationally recognized award-winning architecture and design firm informed by the past to design our shared future. We are technical experts who act as caretakers, conveners, and listeners able to identify the solutions that will best serve the community and long-term life of a place.  Addressing the complex challenges of a variety of project types  from public sites, museums and visitor centers, civic, performing arts, educational, and hospitality, to multi-family housing - we bring expertise in cultural and sustainable stewardship to every decision in our projects and our practice.
Team building is critical to the Quinn Evans practice. We create better work and more fulfilling professional lives together through a culture that fosters constructive engagement with a diversity of staff, practice, client communities, and points of view.
Be a part of a collaborative and diverse team designing ways for places and people to thrive today and tomorrow. As one of the largest women-owned design practices in the country, Quinn Evans takes pride in our diverse team and workplace culture that emphasizes leadership, innovation, teamwork, and professional development. We offer competitive compensation, a supportive hybrid work environment, and opportunity to make a difference through work on unique and impactful projects.

We are seeking a proactive and detail-oriented Human Resources Assistant to join our HR team. The ideal candidate will provide administrative support and assist in various HR functions to ensure smooth and efficient operations within the department. The Human Resources Assistant will play a crucial role in maintaining accurate employee records, coordinating HR processes, and fostering a positive work environment. 
Responsibilities

HR Documentation and Records Management: 

Maintain and update employee files, records, and databases. 
Ensure the accuracy and confidentiality of all employee data, including personal information, benefits, and performance records. 


Employee Onboarding and Offboarding

Coordinate the onboarding process for new hires, including paperwork and orientation sessions. 
Ensure all new hire documents are completed accurately and timely. 
Conduct exit interviews and compile feedback for HR management. 


Recruitment Support: 

Assist in coordinating the recruitment process, including posting job openings, scheduling interviews, and communicating with candidates. 
Prepare and distribute recruitment-related documents, such as offer letters and rejection letters. 


Benefits Administration: 

Provide support in administering employee benefits programs, including health insurance, retirement plans, and leave policies. 
Help employees with inquiries related to benefits and ensure timely resolution. 
Collaborate with benefits providers and HR team to resolve issues. 


HR Policies and Compliance: 

Stay updated on labor laws and company policies to ensure compliance in all HR practices. 
Support HR team in conducting audits to ensure adherence to policies and procedures. 


Employee Relations: 

Assist in resolving employee inquiries and concerns regarding HR-related matters. 
Maintain open communication with employees to foster a positive work environment. 
Address general employee inquiries and direct them to the appropriate channels. 
Escalate employee concerns to HR management when necessary. 


Reporting and Analysis: 

Prepare HR-related reports, including turnover rates, training attendance, and recruitment metrics. 
Assist in analyzing data to identify trends and areas for improvement. 



Requirements

High school diploma or equivalent; relevant college degree preferred. 
Previous experience in an administrative or HR support role is an advantage. 
Knowledge of HR principles and practices is desirable. 
Strong organizational and time management skills. 
Excellent written and verbal communication abilities. 
Ability to maintain confidentiality and handle sensitive information with discretion.
