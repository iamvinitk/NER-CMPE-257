What we do: 
The Test Instrumentation Engineer is responsible for designing and implementing data acquisition systems to support Test  Evaluation projects. This position is a technical contributor supporting ground-based and airborne test platforms. This role is heavily involved in the building of the test platforms and works with the technical engineering teams to collect the test instrumentation requirements and then design a system to accommodate these needs.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Use creativity and engineering principles towards the supervision of installation, maintenance, and upgrade of data acquisition and telemetry systems, including component and sensor selection
Procure, install, maintain, and upgrade test instrumentation and telemetry systems based on a set of given and derived requirements
Work directly with project technical teams to collect and understand the project design requirements
Provide solutions to manufacturing, assembly, and installation issues for data acquisition and telemetry systems
Create and maintain test article instrumentation parameter lists for one or more projects
Solve problematic instrumentation and telemetry components, then develop and complete repairs
Build, maintain, and report instrumentation squawk lists for problematic parameters that cannot be fixed between tests
Represent the TE group in pre- and post-test briefings, reporting to the rest of the team on instrumentation and telemetry statusreadiness
May require up to 30 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned.

What you can contribute:

Bachelors degree in engineering or a related field required
A minimum of three (3) years experience as a Test Engineer (an equivalent combination of education and experience may be considered)
Knowledge of airborne data acquisition systems, e.g. Teletronics (TTC)
Knowledge of various types of transducers, signal conditioning, and data encoders
Knowledge of telemetry systems, e.g. Quasonix
Knowledge of flight test digital data recorders, e.g. Safran (formerly ZodiacHeim)
Knowledge of data processing and storage methods and systems
Knowledge of test methods and lab procedures
Knowledge of Mil Standards, Digital Busses architecture (ARINC, 1553)
Excellent verbal and written communication skills
Proactive delivery of communication and follow up
Excellent organizational skills and attention to detail
Must have the ability to independently prioritize and accomplish work within time constraints
Proficiency with Excel VLOOKUP, pivot tables, MS Office Suite, MS Project

You may also be able to contribute:

Experience using relational databases with some programming skills, SQL, VB, C, and .NET preferred
Hands-on experience in the fabrication and implementation of data acquisition and telemetry systems preferred
