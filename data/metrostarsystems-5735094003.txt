As a Principal Cybersecurity Engineer, youll provide expert support to our government clients cybersecurity initiatives, with the goal of making an impact across the federal government. You will primarily focus on cloud-based services, leading efforts to obtain Authorizations to Operate (ATOs), conducting comprehensive cybersecurity analysis, and providing advanced engineering solutions.

If you think you can see yourself delivering our mission and pursuing our goals with us, then check out the job description below! 
What youll do: 

Provide strategic direction and technical leadership for cybersecurity efforts related to cloud-based services.
Collaborate with cross-functional teams to design and implement secure cloud architectures and solutions.
Lead the ATO process for cloud-based systems, ensuring compliance with relevant security standards and regulations.
Develop and maintain documentation, including System Security Plans (SSPs), Security Assessment Reports (SARs), and Plans of Action and Milestones (POAMs).
Conduct thorough cybersecurity assessments of cloud services, identifying vulnerabilities, risks, and potential threats.
Perform in-depth analysis of security incidents, breaches, and anomalies, providing recommendations for mitigation and prevention.
Design and implement advanced security solutions to protect cloud-based infrastructure, applications, and data.
Develop and deploy security controls, encryption mechanisms, and access management strategies to enhance cloud security.
Serve as a subject matter expert on cloud security best practices, standards, and frameworks.
Provide guidance and mentorship to junior members of the cybersecurity team.
Ensure adherence to relevant cybersecurity regulations, standards, and policies applicable to government clients.
Assist in developing and implementing continuous monitoring processes to sustain compliance post-ATO.
Contribute to the development of incident response plans and playbooks tailored to cloud-based incidents.

What youll need to succeed: 

A Bachelors in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, Information Technology, or a related field.
At least 10 years in cybersecurity roles with a focus on cloud security.
Strong understanding of cloud platforms such as AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud, and associated security controls.
Proven track record of leading ATO processes for complex cloud-based systems and in-depth knowledge of cybersecurity frameworks (NIST, CIS, etc.) and regulatory compliance (FedRAMP, FISMA, etc.).
Hands-on experience with security analysis tools, vulnerability assessment, and penetration testing.
Professional certifications such as CISSP, CCSP, CISM, or equivalent.
Excellent communication and leadership skills, with the ability to collaborate effectively across technical and non-technical teams, along with strong problem-solving skills and the ability to make critical decisions in high-pressure situations.
An Active TSSCI clearance with CI poly preferred or Active TSSCI clearance with ability to pass a CI poly
