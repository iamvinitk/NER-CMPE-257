We are seeking a Senior Commodity Business Manager with a strong background in managing external contract manufacturing for complex instruments to join our growing global Procurement Team. The candidate will be responsible for overseeing the contract manufacturing partners, ensuring the successful production, on-time delivery, high quality and competitive cost of our innovative instruments. 
Individuals applying for this position must be self-starters, strategic thinkers, action driven, flexible in changing direction and methods, thrive in a fast-paced environment, excel at managing multiple priorities, succeed in communicating with all levels within the organization and provide immediate contribution.
 
What you will be doing:

Oversee and foster strong relationships with external contract manufacturing partners to ensure seamless collaboration and mutual success.
Provide critical support during the launch of new instruments at contract manufacturing facilities. Ensure supply chain readiness and facilitate a smooth ramp-up of production.
Define a comprehensive evaluation framework and conduct regular assessments of contract manufacturers to ensure adherence to quality standards, service levels, and pricing competitiveness.
Identify opportunities to enhance manufacturing efficiency and implement process enhancements, driving continuous improvement initiatives.
Conduct regular reviews with contract manufacturers, assess clear-to-build status, and perform risk assessments and management reviews of sub-tier supply chains. Implement effective supply chain risk mitigations.
Play a key role in the Engineering Change Request (ECR) and Engineering Change Order (ECO) process to ensure the smooth roll-out of new changes while minimizing excess and obsolete inventory.
Collaborate both internally and externally to continuously improve first-pass yield, production efficiency, and product quality for contract manufacturing.

 
Minimum Requirements:

Bachelors Degree
8 years of strategic sourcing andor contract manufacturing management experience for instruments
Strong understanding of instrument manufacturing and PCBA processes, quality control methodologies, and supply chain best practices.
Effective communicator with ability to influence at all levels.
Strong business acumen and comfortable to make effective decisions in ambiguity.
Hands-on ability to perform analysis on large amount of data to drive insights and decisions.
Comprehensive knowledge on contract commercial terms and ability to lead supply agreement negotiations independently.
Ability to operate in a fast-paced environment and juggle shifting priorities and tight timelines.

Preferred Skills and Experience:

Bachelors degree in engineering is strongly preferred
