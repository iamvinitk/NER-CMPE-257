Company Description:
We are creating the future of brain-computer interfaces: building devices now that have the potential to help people with paralysis regain mobility and independence and invent new technologies that could expand our abilities, our community, and our world.Team Description: 
As an Engineering Technician at Neuralink for surgery hardware, you will work alongside the mechanical engineering team to tackle mission-critical projects by supporting multiple facets of surgery hardware manufacturing, testing, and use. This includes not only surgical devices, but also surgical proxies, like the one seen here. You will accomplish this by working closely with our hardware designers, machine shop, quality team, and surgery team, making sure that the parts are carefully made, released, and delivered where they are needed. 
As part of this, you will give and receive feedback tofrom designers, surgery staff, and others, as well as identify issues and execute improvement projects. Youll drive efforts like troubleshooting, data analysis, process improvement, and designingbuilding new fixtures. Your projects will immediately impact quality and rate of progress.
Job Responsibilities and Description: 

Assemble a range of medical devices and surgical proxies for both testing and final use
Manage surgery hardware inventory and order requests to facilitate preclinical and clinical study needs
Develop solutions that improve quality, efficiency, and traceability
Work closely with hardware engineers to support testing and issue resolution

Key Qualifications: 

Desire to work with safety-critical assemblies where quality is paramount
Solid understanding of basic physics
Built things that work 

Preferred Qualifications: 

1 year of experience in a manufacturing environment
Experience with Qualification and Acceptance Testing
