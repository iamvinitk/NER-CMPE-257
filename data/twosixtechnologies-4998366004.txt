The Monitoring and Evaluation Specialist will play a critical role in ensuring the effectiveness and impact of a programs initiatives aimed at combating terrorism and other transnational criminal activities. This role is responsible for designing, implementing, and managing monitoring and evaluation (ME) systems, collecting and analyzing data, and providing actionable insights to enhance the programs performance and outcomes. The specialist will work closely with program stakeholders, partners, and beneficiaries to ensure that the programs objectives are met and its impact is maximized.
What you will do:

Develop and Implement ME Framework:

Design and implement a comprehensive monitoring and evaluation framework that aligns with the programs goals and objectives.
Define clear performance indicators, targets, and data collection methods for assessing program effectiveness.


Data Collection and Analysis:

Design and oversee data collection processes to gather relevant information on program activities, outputs, and outcomes.
Collect, compile, and analyze data from various sources to assess the programs impact and identify trends and patterns.
Apply statistical methods and data visualization techniques to present findings effectively.


Performance Assessment:

Conduct regular assessments to measure the programs performance against established indicators and targets.
Identify gaps, challenges, and areas for improvement in program implementation and recommend corrective actions.


Reporting and Documentation:

Prepare regular and ad hoc reports on program performance, utilizing data-driven insights to highlight achievements and areas needing attention.
Maintain comprehensive records of ME activities, methodologies, and findings for accountability and future reference.


Stakeholder Collaboration:

Collaborate closely with program managers, partners, and relevant stakeholders to ensure that ME efforts align with program priorities.
Participate in meetings and workshops to share insights and contribute to strategic planning discussions.


Capacity Building

Provide training and guidance to program staff, partners, and beneficiaries on ME methodologies and best practices.
Foster a culture of learning and continuous improvement within the program.


Learning and Adaptation:

Regularly review ME findings and feedback to inform program adjustments and adaptations.
Propose innovative solutions based on ME insights to enhance program impact and outcomes.



What you will need:

Bachelors or Masters degree in a relevant field (e.g., International Development, Social Sciences, Statistics).
Proven experience (5 years) in designing and implementing ME systems, preferably in international development, security, or counterterrorism programs.
Strong analytical skills and proficiency in data analysis tools and techniques.
Excellent communication and report-writing skills to effectively convey complex information to various stakeholders.
Knowledge of security, law enforcement, and international affairs is desirable.
Proficiency in using ME software and tools.
Ability to work independently, collaborate effectively in a team, and manage multiple tasks simultaneously.
Strong attention to detail, problem-solving skills, and a commitment to quality.
