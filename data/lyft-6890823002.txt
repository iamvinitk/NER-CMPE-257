At Lyft, our mission is to improve peoples lives with the worlds best transportation. To do this, we start with our own community by creating an open, inclusive, and diverse organization.
This position is primarily responsible for facilitating business and legal aspects of producing content at Lyft.Business Affairs generates POs, processes invoices, handles contractsagreements and 3rd-party clearances, estimates talent fees, negotiates licensed music, tracks usage expiration dates, and ensures jobs are actualized and archived. Works closely with Producers, Marketing Program Managers, Creatives, FinanceProcurement, and Legal.  
Responsibilities:

Review creative materials and advise teams on any talent and legal implications and escalate to Lyft counsel as necessary
Prepare talent estimates for production. Advise production on most cost-effective ways  options for hiring talent or securing IP.
Research and negotiate usage rights to third party licensingclearance (image, footage, music, quotes, etc.)
Procure talent - from research on availability and terms, to the final deal memo.  
Process talent payments through session reports, completion of final cast lists, pay and track all talent reuse. Maintain related budget trackers.
Communicate directly with creators and real people talent, to secure releases and issue compensation through third party payroll.
Prepare contracts and SOWs including production contracts, music contracts, talent contracts, agency MSA agreements. Liaise with Lyft legal on redlines as necessary.
Work with internal risk analysts and legal to ensure productions are risk compliant and secure supplemental insurance coverage as necessary.
Additional duties as required.
