What we do: 
The Thermal Systems team is responsible for the thermal management of the energy storage system, electric motor, motor control electronics, air-conditioning, and other LRUs. The Thermal Mechanical Engineer participates in shaping the thermal design of the future of electric aircraft. The role works closely with Battery Research and technology, Dynamic Systems, Airframe, Assembly and integration, and System Safety teams to ensure the thermal systems components are accurately selected, represented, and integrated within the aircraft.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Understand thermal management requirements for safe battery, electric motor, motor control, and drive system thermal performance
Mechanical design and packaging of Thermal Management System components to meet system and aircraft requirements while optimizing for efficiency, cost, weight, manufacturability, and serviceability
Develop technical requirements and specifications for sourced components, and manage system interfaces
Participate in thermal test setup design, running experimental tests, and analyzing test data
Model the coolant network including aircraft components, routing, valves, pumps, tanks, heat exchangers, and our primary heat rejection and storage devices
Develop compact heat rejection devices for sub-system components including cold plates and heat sinks, and their interfaces to heat sources and the thermal management system
Define and mature mechanical and fluid interfaces with aircraft systems to include airframe, transmissions, battery system, and motor and motor control systems
Collaborate with supplierspartners to ensure program milestones are achieved
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned
