Supernal is at the forefront of creating emerging mobility solutions that will foster the development of human-centered cities. We are designing a completely new electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft tailored to the mobility needs of future cities. This allows passengers a seamless intermodal journey that safely transports them to their final destination. We fuse research in autonomy, robotics, aviation and services to define a new category of mobility for the worlds communities. We believe in creative thinking and collaboration to help build a better mobility experience for everyone, improving peoples ability to move  whether for work or play.
What we do: 
The Head of Propulsion  Dynamic Systems is responsible for leading the development and integration of eVTOL propulsion and rotary system to achieve functional and performance requirements of the aircraft. The role works with cutting edge technologies including high voltage architectures, traction motors, power electronics and powertrain controls to meet customer requirements, ensure corporate objectives, and ensures designs are safe, certifiable and can be manufactured and produced economically.
This position is responsible for building the team and for the technical and managerial oversight of key capabilities and performance requirements for multiple aircraft including development and integration of innovative propulsion architectures and system designs to achieve functional requirements, architecture design with optimized redundancy levels; specification of hardware elements and technical support to procurement; propulsion software specification; fault management design; lab testing of flight hardware  software; on-aircraft testing; support to vehicle flight testing; and anomaly resolution among other responsibilities. The role partners with energy storage systems, flight controls, and others for powertrain control development to ensure designs can be manufactured and produced safely and economically.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
