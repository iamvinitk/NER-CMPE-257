A leader in autonomous driving since 2007, Torc has spent over a decade commercializing our solutions with experienced partners. Now a part of the Daimler family, we are focused solely on developing software for automated trucks to transform how the world moves freight.
Join us and catapult your career with the company that helped pioneer autonomous technology, and the first AV software company with the vision to partner directly with a truck manufacturer.Meet the Team: 
The VP, Safety  Regulatory Policy  Assurance is a highly experienced and respected safety leader responsible for assuring the safety of Torcs product development and launch readiness. This leadership role requires broad safety knowledge and safety case experience to champion the safety and regulatory product strategic plan and guide its execution.  An important part of this role is building credibility with federal, state, and local government stakeholders to position Torc as a trusted partner. As a safety leader for Torc, they understand the safety and regulatory environment including how it may impact Torcs technology and business priorities. They work to gain cross-functional alignment on policies to advance Torcs business priorities leveraging the Daimler network and collaborating to influence outcomes more effectively.
As VP, SR Policy  Assurance, they will need to build and manage a highly effective and experienced Safety  Regulatory Assurance and Policy team.  This team works with the Product Team to establish safety requirements that guide the design of a safe product and when necessary, works with the Legal Team to provide regulatory interpretations for the development of functional requirements. They also oversee and assess the evidence from Engineering and other teams on the delivery of the safety case, conformance with safety standards (e.g., functional safety, the safety of the intended functionality, and cyber security), and compliance with regulations.  One of the critical aspects for success in this role will be working collaboratively with business partners (e.g., Product  Strategy, Engineering, Legal, Fleet Ops, and People Ops) to establish and refine the safety processes, interfaces, and safety release gateways for deployment.
In addition to collaborating internally, this role will need to build relationships across the industry. The Safety  Regulatory Assurance and Policy team will lead the external interfaces with government agencies, industry consortiums, and research groups. Determining the strategy for engagement and guiding company representatives in collaboration with the Marketing and Communication team to help ensure the engagement delivers the desired results.   They will need to monitor the safety and regulatory environment, communicate the potential impact to Torc, and work with business partners to try to shape and develop business contingency plans
What youll do: 

Champions the strategic plan for the Torc Safety Case including alignment with business partners and manages the delivery of the plan
Chairs the Legal and Regulatory Safety (LARS) committee and leads the safety processes for providing the Product team with the safety requirements.
Establish a strategy related to safety standards and processes for assessing or auditing conformance to key industry standards and guide the team to in the execution and conformance assessment
Monitors and where appropriate, active engagement in the development of safety standards and industry best practices by participation in industry technical working groups and committees, including SAE, ISO, UL, and the AVSC.
Guides their team to oversee the execution of Torcs Regulatory Strategic Plan and delivery of a compliant product.
Leads research and rulemaking engagements with state and federal regulators, including state DOTs, NHTSA, and FMCSA.
Leads government relations with federal and state representatives and collaboration with Daimlers government affairs team.
Forecasts and communicates present and future risks and opportunities as policy, regulations, and standards evolve.
Leads the strategic vision and purpose for the Safety  Regulatory Assurance and Policy team.
Leads the development of a well-respected team internal and external to Torc.
In partnership with People Operations, supervisory responsibilities include leading team goal setting, career path planning and professional development, tracking team competencies, employee team building and training, performance management, work prioritization, and resource management.
Accomplish department objectives by supervising employees, overseeing work direction and progress, and working collaboratively with the larger Safety  Regulatory organization and company.

10 years experience in automotive safety and regulatory
Proven-track record for delivering safety programs
Demonstrated ability to lead and build an effective and well-respected team

Understanding of vehicle design and operation, including powertrain controls, electrical architecture, and vehicle dynamics.
