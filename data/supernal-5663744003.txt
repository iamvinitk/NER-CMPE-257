What we do: 
The Civil Certification Engineer  Electrical Systems  Powertrain is responsible for serving as the technical point of contact between the electrical engineering disciplines (powertrain, energy storage, etc) and the certification department.
This position will be required to work on-site 5 days a week.
What you can do:

Support the senior civil certification engineers at the project-level and support certification initiatives as assigned
Maintain awareness of new regulatory requirements and guidance materials as they are released
Liaise between the certification and engineering teams, to include: coordinating certification requirements updates to the engineering team and providing certification inputs as part of the design process. As well as, communicating any design changes or proposed design changes with potential certification impacts back to the certification team
Support technical meetings between FAA and internal team members as they relate to electrical systems certification
Participate in applicable industry working groups
Support cross-functional disciplines
Provide technical inputs and checks of certain certification documents
May require up to 10 of domestic and international travel
Other duties as assigned

What you can contribute:

Working knowledge of industry standards related to aircraft electrical systems and components (SAE, RTCA, EUROCAE, etc.)
Experience with 14 CFR airworthiness requirements (Parts 23, 25, 27, andor 29)
Experience with document and data organizationmanagement
