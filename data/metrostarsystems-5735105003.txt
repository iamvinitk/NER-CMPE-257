What youll do: 

 Collaborate with cross-functional teams and government stakeholders to gather requirements and design scalable IdAM solutions that align with government security standards and business objectives.
Implement, configure, and manage identity and access management technologies, ensuring compliance with government regulations and security protocols. This includes Single Sign-On (SSO), Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), and Identity Federation.
Develop and maintain authentication and authorization processes that meet the stringent security requirements of government contracts while providing a user-friendly experience.
Design and enforce access control policies, monitoring mechanisms, and security protocols to safeguard sensitive data and resources, meeting or exceeding government security mandates.
Perform regular assessments and audits of IdAM systems to identify vulnerabilities, recommend improvements, and ensure compliance with government regulations such as FISMA, FedRAMP, or DoD RMF.
Collaborate with the security team to respond to and remediate security incidents in alignment with government incident response procedures.
Maintain detailed documentation of system configurations, procedures, and compliance efforts for government audits.

What youll need to succeed: 

8 years of proven experience working as an Identity and Access Management Engineer, with a demonstrated track record of supporting government clients.
In-depth understanding of identity management principles, authentication protocols (SAML, OAuth, OpenID Connect), and government-specific authorization mechanisms.
Extensive experience with enterprise IdAM solutions, such as Microsoft Azure AD, Okta, Ping Identity, or similar platforms, in a government context.
Strong knowledge of government security frameworks and regulations (NIST SP 800-53, FISMA, FedRAMP, DoD RMF).
Proficiency in scripting languages (Python, PowerShell, etc.) for automation and integration, particularly in government environments.
Excellent problem-solving skills and the ability to navigate complex technical challenges while adhering to government security requirements.
Effective communication skills to collaborate with cross-functional teams, government stakeholders, and non-technical personnel.
Relevant industry certifications such as CISSP, CISM, or CompTIA Security are advantageous.
Relevant government-specific certifications (e.g., DoD 8570) and an active TSSCI clearance with CI poly preferred or Active TSSCI clearance with the ability to pass a CI poly
Experience with cloud environments (AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, etc.) and containerization technologies (Docker, Kubernetes) for government clients is a plus.
